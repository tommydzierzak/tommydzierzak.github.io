Notable projects I've worked on

Mechatronics and Instrumentation Lab

This course provided an integrated theoretical and experimental learning experience in the area of electrical and mechanical systems. A great focus of this class was gaining experience using electronic devices that would allow us to collect mechanical data and analyze it. We also had much experience working with other engineers and writing technical reports, as we collected data and wrote a report with new, random partners every week.

Our final project for the class was to determine the unknown parameters: mass, damping, spring constant, resistance, inductance, capacitance, and magnetic flux coupling coefficient.

Vibrations

This course served as an intermediate step after our initial circuits course to prepare us theoretically for Mechatronics and Instrumentation. It focused on Modeling and Analysis of dynamic systems using Newton's second law and Lagrange methods as well as developing and solving equations of motion. Students also formulated and solved free and forced vibration problems for damped and undamped systems in both time and frequency domain.

Our final project for the class was to model graphically an electro-mechanical system. The system chosen was the Aura Virtual Reality vest, essentially an electronically excited MCK system.

Numerical Techniques

This course was an extension of all of the MatLab courses we had taken previously. Students practiced applying Matlab to complex differential equations, writing easy to use functions, and evaluating eigenvectors.

The final project for this course was very open ended, we just had to create a project that incorporated a topic learned in class. Me and a couple other classmates decided to attempt photo recognition using the eigenface method for facial recognition. Our code 'learns a face' by taking 100 sample pictures and is able to 'compare a face' by taking another picture and comparing it to the 'learned face'.
